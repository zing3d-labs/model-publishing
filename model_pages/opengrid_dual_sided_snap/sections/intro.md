A double-sided openGrid snap connector — one snap on each face, back to back. Use it to join two openGrid tiles together, mount a basket to a wall panel, or connect any two grid surfaces at the same location.

Each side is independently configurable: Standard (6.8mm) or Lite (3.4mm) thickness, and Nondirectional or Directional engagement. Mix and match to suit whatever grids you're connecting.

## When to Use Each Option

**Standard vs Lite:** Match the thickness to the grid tile on that side. Use Standard for Full (6.8mm) tiles and Lite for Lite (3.4mm) tiles. Mixing is fine — for example, Lite on side A and Standard on side B to bridge a Lite basket wall to a Full mounting panel.

**Nondirectional vs Directional:** Nondirectional snaps insert and release from any direction. Directional snaps lock in from one side only, providing a stronger hold that resists pull-out. Use directional on sides where the connection needs to stay secure under load, such as a wall-mounted basket.

## Detachable Panel Mounting

Side B is flipped 180° relative to Side A, which enables a useful detachment behavior when using directional snaps.

When mounting a movable panel (basket, board, cover) to a fixed wall grid:

- Use a **directional snap on Side B** (the movable panel side). Because Side B is flipped, its directional orientation faces downward — locking the snap firmly to the movable panel and resisting pull-out in that direction.
- Use a **directional snap on Side A** (the wall side). When you tilt the movable panel outward to remove it, the tilt direction is the release direction for Side A's snap — so it releases from the wall cleanly.

The result: tilting the panel detaches it from the wall, but the snap stays behind on the movable panel rather than on the wall grid. This makes reassembly straightforward — the snap is always with the thing you're carrying.
